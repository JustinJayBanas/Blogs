Week 1 of February

Day 1: Our internship began with an orientation led by the dean. Ate Ella guided us to our assigned department, BU-USDS, where we met our mentor. Our team has two IT students and one CBEM student. Our mentor explained our tasks, which include fixing their system and adding new features.
Day 2: We helped renovate tables and organize documents in the office. After that, we started checking the spreadsheet monitoring system to find what needed fixing.
Day 3: There was a power outage, so Sir Ronaldo gave me clerical work. I checked documents to make sure they were complete and organized.

Week 2 of February

We worked on the spreadsheet monitoring system and used Google Apps Script to fix errors and enhance its performance. The BU-USDS team tested it and reported that it was faster and had fewer mistakes. Another task assigned to us was to thoroughly check documents for student travel, on-the-job training (OJT), and student activities to ensure they were complete and organized.

Week 3 of February

This week, we decided to tackle the dashboard of the spreadsheet monitoring system and used Google Apps Script to optimize its performance. Fixing the dashboard itself was challenging, as it’s an outdated system with some errors, and we’re uncertain about how previous OJT students designed it. As part of our weekly responsibilities, we thoroughly checked documents for student travel, on-the-job training (OJT), and student activities to ensure they were complete and well-organized.

Week 4 of February

This week, we made a bold decision to move away from the spreadsheet and began building a web-based monitoring system, a direction our mentor fully supported as a more effective solution. We developed a simple prototype using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, connected to a database for improved functionality. Randiel took the lead on constructing the web-based system, as I was assigned other tasks. As part of our weekly responsibilities, we thoroughly checked documents for student travel, on-the-job training (OJT), and student activities to ensure they were complete and well-organized.
